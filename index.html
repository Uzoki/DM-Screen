    Cover

    Half Cover = +2 AC and Dexterity Saving Throw

    Three-Quarters Cover = +5 AC and Dexterity Saving Throw

    Total Cover = Can't be targeted directly

    Obscured

    Lightly Obscured = Disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight

    Heavily Obscured = You have the Blinded condition while trying to see something in the area

    Conditions
    Blinded
    New Rules (2024)
    • You can't see and automatically fail any ability check that requires sight.
    • Attack rolls against you have Advantage, and your attack rolls have Disadvantage.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A blinded creature can't see and automatically fails any ability check that requires sight.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage, and the creature's attack rolls have disadvantage.
    Charmed
    New Rules (2024)
    • You can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with damaging abilities or magical effects.
    • The charmer has Advantage on any ability check to interact with you socially.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A charmed creature can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects.
    • The charmer has advantage on any ability check to interact socially with the creature.
    Deafened
    New Rules (2024)
    • You can't hear and automatically fail any ability check that requires hearing.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A deafened creature can't hear and automatically fails any ability check that requires hearing.
    Exhaustion
    New Rules (2024)
    • Exhaustion is cumulative and measured in levels, from 1 to 6.
    • Each level gives you a cumulative −2 penalty to every d20 roll (ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws).
    • Each level also reduces your Speed by 5 feet, cumulative.
    • If your Exhaustion level reaches 6, you die.
    • Finishing a long rest removes 1 Exhaustion level.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • Exhaustion is cumulative and measured in levels, from 1 to 6, with effects that stack as the level increases.
    • Level 1: Disadvantage on ability checks.
    • Level 2: Speed halved.
    • Level 3: Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws.
    • Level 4: Hit point maximum halved.
    • Level 5: Speed reduced to 0.
    • Level 6: Death.
    • Finishing a long rest reduces Exhaustion by 1 level.

    Double-check this one against your books if you can — it's rebuilt from research rather than copied straight from an official source like the rest of this list.

    Frightened
    New Rules (2024)
    • You have Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is within line of sight.
    • You can't willingly move closer to the source of fear.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A frightened creature has disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of its fear is within line of sight.
    • The creature can't willingly move closer to the source of its fear.
    Grappled
    New Rules (2024)
    • Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.
    • You have Disadvantage on attack rolls against any target other than the grappler.
    • The grappler can drag or carry you when it moves, but every foot of movement costs it 1 extra foot unless you are Tiny or two or more sizes smaller than it.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A grappled creature's speed becomes 0, and it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed.
    • The condition ends if the grappler is incapacitated (see the condition).
    • The condition also ends if an effect removes the grappled creature from the reach of the grappler or grappling effect, such as when a creature is hurled away by the thunderwave spell.
    Incapacitated
    New Rules (2024)
    • You can't take any action, Bonus Action, or Reaction.
    • Your Concentration is broken.
    • You can't speak.
    • If you're Incapacitated when you roll Initiative, you have Disadvantage on the roll.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • An incapacitated creature can't take actions or reactions.
    Invisible
    New Rules (2024)
    • If you're Invisible when you roll Initiative, you have Advantage on the roll.
    • You aren't affected by any effect that requires its target to be seen, unless the effect's creator can somehow see you. Any equipment you're wearing or carrying is also concealed.
    • Attack rolls against you have Disadvantage, and your attack rolls have Advantage, unless the attacker can somehow see you.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • An invisible creature is impossible to see without the aid of magic or a special sense. For the purpose of hiding, the creature is heavily obscured. The creature's location can be detected by any noise it makes or any tracks it leaves.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have disadvantage, and the creature's attack rolls have advantage.
    Paralyzed
    New Rules (2024)
    • You have the Incapacitated condition.
    • Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.
    • You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • Attack rolls against you have Advantage.
    • Any attack roll that hits you is a Critical Hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of you.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A paralyzed creature is incapacitated (see the condition) and can't move or speak.
    • The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
    • Any attack that hits the creature is a critical hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.
    Petrified
    New Rules (2024)
    • You're transformed, along with any nonmagical objects you're wearing and carrying, into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Your weight increases by a factor of ten, and you cease aging.
    • You have the Incapacitated condition.
    • Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.
    • Attack rolls against you have Advantage.
    • You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • You have Resistance to all damage.
    • You have Immunity to the Poisoned condition (note: as of 2024 you are no longer immune to disease while petrified).
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A petrified creature is transformed, along with any nonmagical object it is wearing or carrying, into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Its weight increases by a factor of ten, and it ceases aging.
    • The creature is incapacitated (see the condition), can't move or speak, and is unaware of its surroundings.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
    • The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • The creature has resistance to all damage.
    • The creature is immune to poison and disease, although a poison or disease already in its system is suspended, not neutralized.
    Poisoned
    New Rules (2024)
    • You have Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A poisoned creature has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
    Prone
    New Rules (2024)
    • Your only movement options are to crawl, or to spend an amount of movement equal to half your Speed (round down) to right yourself and end the condition. If your Speed is 0, you can't right yourself.
    • You have Disadvantage on attack rolls. An attack roll against you has Advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, that attack roll has Disadvantage.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A prone creature's only movement option is to crawl, unless it stands up and thereby ends the condition.
    • The creature has disadvantage on attack rolls.
    • An attack roll against the creature has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
    Restrained
    New Rules (2024)
    • Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.
    • Attack rolls against you have Advantage, and your attack rolls have Disadvantage.
    • You have Disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A restrained creature's speed becomes 0, and it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage, and the creature's attack rolls have disadvantage.
    • The creature has disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws.
    Stunned
    New Rules (2024)
    • You have the Incapacitated condition.
    • You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • Attack rolls against you have Advantage.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • A stunned creature is incapacitated (see the condition), can't move, and can speak only falteringly.
    • The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.
    Unconscious
    New Rules (2024)
    • You have the Incapacitated and Prone conditions, and you drop whatever you're holding. When this condition ends, you remain Prone.
    • Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.
    • Attack rolls against you have Advantage.
    • You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • Any attack roll that hits you is a Critical Hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of you.
    • You're unaware of your surroundings.
    Legacy Rules (2014)
    • An unconscious creature is incapacitated, can't move or speak, and is unaware of its surroundings.
    • The creature drops whatever it's holding and falls prone.
    • The creature automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws.
    • Attack rolls against the creature have advantage.

    Ability Check

    A D20 Test that represents using one of the six abilities — or a specific skill associated with an ability — to overcome a challenge.

    Ability Score and Modifier

    A creature has six ability scores — Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma — each with a corresponding modifier. Add the modifier when making a D20 Test with that ability, or whenever a rule says to.

    Action

    On your turn you can take one action. The special named actions (Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Influence, Magic, Ready, Search, Study, Utilize) each have their own entry below, in addition to whatever special actions your features provide.

    Advantage

    If you have Advantage on a D20 Test, roll two d20s and use the higher roll. A roll can't be affected by more than one Advantage, and Advantage and Disadvantage on the same roll cancel each other out.

    Adventure

    A series of encounters. A story emerges through playing them.

    Alignment

    A creature's alignment broadly describes its ethical attitudes and ideals: one factor identifies morality (good, evil, or neutral), the other describes attitude toward order (lawful, chaotic, or neutral) — nine possible combinations in total, such as Lawful Good or Neutral Evil.

    Ally

    A creature is your ally if it's a member of your adventuring party, your friend, on your side in combat, or designated as your ally by the rules or the DM.

    Area of Effect

    Many spells and features have an area of effect, typically one of six shapes: Cone, Cube, Cylinder, Emanation, Line, or Sphere (each has its own entry below).

    An area of effect has a point of origin, from which its energy erupts. If every straight line from the point of origin to a location in the area is blocked by an obstruction giving Total Cover, that location isn't included. If the origin point is placed unseen behind an obstruction such as a wall, the origin instead forms on the near side of it.

    Armor Class

    The target number for an attack roll — how difficult a target is to hit. Base AC is 10 plus your Dexterity modifier, unless a rule gives you another base AC calculation; you can only use one such calculation at a time.

    Armor Training

    Lets you use armor of a certain category without penalty. Wearing Light, Medium, or Heavy armor without training gives Disadvantage on any Strength or Dexterity D20 Test and prevents casting spells. Using a Shield without training means you don't gain its AC bonus.

    Attack [Action]

    Lets you make one attack roll with a weapon or an Unarmed Strike.

    • Equipping/Unequipping. You can equip or unequip one weapon as part of this action, before or after the attack, without needing to use it for that attack.
    • Moving Between Attacks. If a feature such as Extra Attack gives you more than one attack, you can split your movement between those attacks.
    Attack Roll

    A D20 Test representing an attack with a weapon, an Unarmed Strike, or a spell.

    Attitude

    A monster's starting attitude toward a player character: Friendly, Hostile, or Indifferent.

    Attunement

    Some magic items require forming a bond — Attunement — before their magical properties can be used. A creature can be attuned to no more than three magic items at a time.

    Blindsight

    Lets you see within a set range without relying on physical sight. You can see anything not behind Total Cover in that range even if Blinded or in Darkness, and you can see creatures with the Invisible condition within that range.

    Bloodied

    A creature is Bloodied while it has half its Hit Points or fewer remaining.

    Bonus Action

    A special action you can take on the same turn as your action. You can't take more than one Bonus Action per turn, and only when a rule explicitly grants one.

    Bright Light

    Normal illumination.

    Burning [Hazard]

    A burning creature or object takes 1d4 Fire damage at the start of each of its turns. As an action, a creature can extinguish itself by dropping Prone and rolling on the ground. Fire also goes out if doused, submerged, or suffocated.

    Burrow Speed

    Lets a creature move through sand, earth, mud, or ice. It can't burrow through solid rock unless a trait says otherwise.

    Campaign

    A series of adventures.

    Cantrip

    A level 0 spell, cast without using a spell slot.

    Carrying Capacity

    Your size and Strength score determine how much weight you can carry, drag, lift, or push. While dragging, lifting, or pushing more than you can carry, your Speed can be no more than 5 feet.

    Challenge Rating

    Summarizes the threat a monster poses to four player characters. Higher than party level suggests danger, lower suggests little threat — but circumstances and party size can change this significantly in play.

    Character Sheet

    A paper or digital record used to track your character's information.

    Climbing

    Each foot of movement while climbing costs 1 extra foot (2 extra in Difficult Terrain) unless you have and use a Climb Speed. The DM may require a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check on a slippery surface or one with few handholds.

    Climb Speed

    Used in place of Speed to climb a vertical surface without the extra movement cost normally required.

    Concentration

    Some spells and effects require Concentration to stay active. It can be ended voluntarily at any time (no action). It's broken by any of the following:

    • Another Concentration Effect. Starting to cast a Concentration spell, or activating another Concentration effect, ends the previous one.
    • Damage. Taking damage requires a Constitution save (DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher, max DC 30) to maintain it.
    • Incapacitated or Dead. Concentration ends immediately if you're Incapacitated or die.
    Condition

    A temporary game state that affects its recipient in a defined way. A condition doesn't stack with itself — a creature either has it or doesn't — except Exhaustion, which is cumulative. See the Conditions dropdown above for the full list.

    Cone [Area of Effect]

    Extends in straight lines from a point of origin in a chosen direction. Its width at any point equals that point's distance from the origin (e.g. 15 feet wide at 15 feet from the origin). The origin itself isn't included in the area unless its creator says otherwise.

    Crawling

    Each foot of movement while crawling costs 1 extra foot (2 extra in Difficult Terrain).

    Creature

    Any being in the game, including a player character, is a creature.

    Creature Type

    Every creature has a tag identifying its type (most player characters are Humanoid). Types have no rules of their own, but some rules affect certain types differently. See the Creature Types by Skill dropdown above.

    Critical Hit

    Rolling a natural 20 on an attack roll scores a Critical Hit, which always hits regardless of AC or modifiers. Roll all the attack's damage dice twice and add them together, then add relevant modifiers.

    Cube [Area of Effect]

    Extends in straight lines from a point of origin located anywhere on one of its faces. The effect that creates it specifies the length of each side. The origin isn't included in the area unless its creator says otherwise.

    Curses

    The effect that confers a curse defines what it does. Curses can be removed by Remove Curse, Greater Restoration, or other magic that explicitly ends curses.

    Cylinder [Area of Effect]

    Extends in straight lines from a point of origin at the center of the circular top or bottom face. The effect specifies the base radius and the height. Unlike most shapes, the origin point IS included in the area.

    D20 Test

    Covers the three main d20 rolls: ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws. Anything affecting D20 Tests affects all three. The DM decides when a D20 Test is warranted.

    Damage

    Represents harm that causes a creature or object to lose Hit Points.

    Damage Roll

    A die roll, adjusted by applicable modifiers, that deals damage to a target.

    Damage Threshold

    A creature or object with a damage threshold has Immunity to all damage unless a single attack or effect deals damage equal to or greater than the threshold, in which case it takes the full amount. Damage that fails to meet the threshold is superficial and does nothing.

    Damage Types

    Acid, Bludgeoning, Cold, Fire, Force, Lightning, Necrotic, Piercing, Poison, Psychic, Radiant, Slashing, Thunder. Damage types carry no rules of their own, but other rules (like Resistance) key off them.

    Darkness

    An area of Darkness is Heavily Obscured.

    Darkvision

    Lets you see in Dim Light within a set range as if it were Bright Light, and in Darkness within that range as if it were Dim Light (colors appear only as shades of gray in that Darkness).

    Dash [Action]

    Gives extra movement equal to your Speed (after modifiers) for the current turn. You may use a special speed (like Fly Speed) instead of your normal Speed, chosen each time you Dash.

    Dead

    A dead creature has no Hit Points and can't regain them without magic like Raise Dead or Revivify — the spirit must consent, having departed for the Outer Planes. On revival it keeps ongoing conditions, contagions, or curses (if durations haven't lapsed), returns with 1 fewer Exhaustion level if it died with any, and loses any Attunement it had.

    Death Saving Throw

    A player character must make a Death Save if they start their turn at 0 Hit Points.

    Dehydration [Hazard]

    Drinking less than half a day's required water gives 1 Exhaustion level at day's end, which can't be removed until a full day's water is drunk.

    Difficult Terrain

    Every foot of movement in Difficult Terrain costs 1 extra foot (not cumulative — a space either is or isn't Difficult Terrain). A space qualifies if it contains, among other things: a non-Tiny non-ally creature, furniture sized for your size or larger, heavy snow/ice/rubble/undergrowth, liquid shin-to-waist deep, a narrow opening one size smaller than you, or a slope of 20 degrees or more.

    Difficulty Class

    The target number (DC) for an ability check or saving throw.

    Dim Light

    An area of Dim Light is Lightly Obscured.

    Disadvantage

    If you have Disadvantage on a D20 Test, roll two d20s and use the lower roll. A roll can't be affected by more than one Disadvantage, and Advantage/Disadvantage on the same roll cancel out.

    Disengage [Action]

    Your movement doesn't provoke Opportunity Attacks for the rest of the turn.

    Dodge [Action]

    Until the start of your next turn, attack rolls against you have Disadvantage if you can see the attacker, and you make Dexterity saves with Advantage. Lost if you're Incapacitated or your Speed is 0.

    Emanation [Area of Effect]

    Extends in straight lines from a creature or object in all directions, out to a distance the effect specifies. It moves with its origin unless instantaneous or stationary. The origin itself isn't included in the area unless its creator says otherwise.

    Encounter

    A scene in an adventure belonging to at least one of the game's three pillars: social interaction, exploration, or combat.

    Enemy

    A creature is your enemy if it fights against you, actively works to harm you, or is designated your enemy by the rules or DM.

    Experience Points

    Awarded by the DM for overcoming challenges and completing adventures. Crossing certain XP thresholds increases a character's level.

    Expertise

    Doubles your Proficiency Bonus on an ability check using a skill proficiency you have Expertise in (unless already doubled by another feature). Expertise is gained in a skill you're already proficient in, and can't stack twice in the same skill.

    Falling [Hazard]

    A fall deals 1d6 Bludgeoning damage at the end of the fall per 10 feet fallen, max 20d6, and leaves you Prone on landing unless you take no damage. Falling into liquid lets you use your Reaction for a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check to hit head/feet first, halving the damage on a success.

    Flying

    While flying, you fall if Incapacitated, Prone, or your Fly Speed drops to 0 — unless you can hover.

    Fly Speed

    Lets you travel through the air. You can stay aloft until you land, fall, or die.

    Friendly [Attitude]

    A Friendly creature views you favorably; you have Advantage on ability checks to influence it.

    Grappling

    Characters typically grapple with an Unarmed Strike; some monsters have special grapple attacks.

    • Grappled Condition. A successful grapple gives the target the Grappled condition.
    • One Grapple per Hand. A free hand (or equivalent body part) is needed, and it can grapple only one creature at a time.
    • Ending a Grapple. The target can use its action for a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against the escape DC. It also ends if the grappler is Incapacitated, if the target is moved beyond the grapple's range, or if the grappler releases it (no action required).
    Hazard

    An environmental danger — see Burning, Dehydration, Falling, Malnutrition, and Suffocation.

    Healing

    How you regain Hit Points.

    Heavily Obscured

    You have the Blinded condition while trying to see something in a Heavily Obscured space.

    Help [Action]
    • Assist an Ability Check. Choose a skill/tool proficiency and a nearby ally; they get Advantage on the next check with that skill/tool before the start of your next turn. DM has final say on feasibility.
    • Assist an Attack Roll. Distract an enemy within 5 feet, giving an ally Advantage on their next attack against it before the start of your next turn.
    Heroic Inspiration

    Can be expended to reroll any die immediately after rolling it (must use the new result). Gaining it while you already have it is lost unless given to a player character who lacks it.

    Hide [Action]

    Requires a successful DC 15 Dexterity (Stealth) check while Heavily Obscured or behind Three-Quarters/Total Cover, and out of any enemy's line of sight. On success, you have the Invisible condition while hidden; the check total becomes the DC for others to find you via Wisdom (Perception).

    Hiding ends immediately if you make a sound louder than a whisper, an enemy finds you, you make an attack roll, or you cast a spell with a Verbal component.

    High Jump

    Jump vertically 3 + Strength modifier feet (min 0) with a 10-foot running start; half that standing still. Each foot costs a foot of movement. You can reach half your height higher during the jump, for a total reach of the jump height plus 1.5x your height.

    Hit Point Dice

    Help determine a character's HP maximum. Most monsters have them too. Can be spent during a Short Rest to regain HP.

    Hit Points

    A measure of how hard a creature or object is to kill or destroy. Damage reduces HP, healing restores it, and it can never exceed the maximum or drop below 0.

    Hostile [Attitude]

    A Hostile creature views you unfavorably; you have Disadvantage on ability checks to influence it.

    Hover

    Some creatures, spells, or effects grant the ability to hover, which prevents falling while flying in certain circumstances.

    Illusions

    The effect that creates an illusion defines what it does and which senses it deceives. An illusion is insubstantial and weightless but seems affected by its environment (casting shadows, echoing, etc.) unless stated otherwise.

    Immunity

    If you have Immunity to a damage type or condition, it doesn't affect you at all.

    Improvised Weapons

    An object wielded as a makeshift weapon (broken glass, a table leg, etc.). A Simple/Martial weapon used contrary to its design (e.g. a ranged weapon in melee, or throwing a non-Thrown melee weapon) also counts as improvised.

    • Proficiency. No Proficiency Bonus applies.
    • Damage. 1d4 of a type the DM deems appropriate on a hit.
    • Range. If thrown: normal range 20 feet, long range 60 feet.
    • Weapon Equivalents. The DM may rule that an improvised weapon resembling a real one uses that weapon's rules.
    Indifferent [Attitude]

    An Indifferent creature has no desire to help or hinder you — the default attitude of a monster.

    Influence [Action]

    Urge a monster to do something. The DM decides whether the monster is Willing (no check, it complies its own way), Unwilling (no check, it refuses), or Hesitant (requires an ability check).

    For a Hesitant response, the DC is 15 or the monster's Intelligence score, whichever is higher. Success means it does as urged; failure means you must wait 24 hours (or DM-set duration) to try the same approach again.

    Initiative

    Determines turn order in combat. A DM may instead use a fixed Initiative score of 10 + Dexterity modifier, +5 if Advantage on Initiative rolls, -5 if Disadvantage.

    Jumping

    A jump is either a Long Jump (horizontal) or a High Jump (vertical) — see their own entries, or the Jumping calculator dropdown above.

    Knocking Out a Creature

    When a melee attack would reduce a creature to 0 HP, you can instead reduce it to 1 HP; it gains the Unconscious condition and starts a Short Rest. It stays Unconscious until it regains HP or someone succeeds on a DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check to administer first aid.

    Lightly Obscured

    Disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks to see something in a Lightly Obscured space.

    Line [Area of Effect]

    Extends from a point of origin in a straight path, covering an area defined by its length and width. The origin isn't included unless its creator says otherwise.

    Long Jump

    Leap horizontally up to your Strength score in feet with a 10-foot running start; half that standing still. Each foot costs a foot of movement.

    Landing in Difficult Terrain requires a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or you fall Prone. At the DM's option, clearing a low obstacle (no taller than a quarter of the jump distance) requires a DC 10 Strength (Athletics) check, or you hit it.

    Long Rest

    At least 8 hours of downtime with at least 6 hours of sleep and no more than 2 hours of light activity. You must have at least 1 HP to start one, and must wait 16 hours after finishing one before starting another.

    Benefits: regain all HP and spent Hit Point Dice (and any reduced HP maximum returns to normal), reduced ability scores are restored, Exhaustion level decreases by 1, and any features that recharge on a Long Rest do so.

    Interruptions: rolling Initiative, casting a non-cantrip spell, taking any damage, or an hour of exertion — each stops the rest. If at least 1 hour had passed, you gain a Short Rest's benefits instead. Resuming after an interruption adds 1 extra hour per interruption.

    Magic [Action]

    Cast a spell with a casting time of an action, or activate a feature/item that requires this action. Spells with a casting time of 1 minute or longer require taking the Magic action (with Concentration) on every turn of casting; if Concentration breaks, the spell fails without expending a slot.

    Magical Effect

    An effect is magical if created by a spell, magic item, or a phenomenon a rule labels magical.

    Malnutrition [Hazard]

    Eating less than half a day's required food requires a DC 10 Constitution save or gain 1 Exhaustion level at day's end. Eating nothing for 5 days automatically gives 1 Exhaustion level at day 5, plus another for each additional day without food. This Exhaustion can't be removed until a full day's food is eaten.

    Monster

    A creature controlled by the DM, even if benevolent.

    Nonplayer Character

    An NPC is a monster with a personal name and a distinct personality.

    Object

    A nonliving, distinct thing. Composite things (like buildings) comprise more than one object.

    Occupied Space

    A space is occupied if a creature is in it, or it's completely filled by objects.

    Opportunity Attacks

    Triggered when a creature you can see leaves your reach via its action, Bonus Action, Reaction, or a speed. Use your Reaction to make one melee attack against it, occurring right before it leaves your reach.

    Passive Perception

    Reflects general awareness without an active check: 10 + Wisdom (Perception) check bonus, +5 with Advantage on such checks, -5 with Disadvantage.

    Per Day

    A "per day" use requires finishing a Long Rest to use again once exhausted.

    Player Character

    A character controlled by a player.

    Possession

    A possessing effect defines how the possession works. Prevented by Protection from Evil and Good, and ended by Dispel Evil and Good.

    Proficiency

    Add your Proficiency Bonus to a D20 Test made with something you're proficient in — a skill, saving throw, weapon, or tool.

    Reach

    A creature has 5 feet of reach unless a rule says otherwise.

    Reaction

    A special action taken in response to a defined trigger, usable on another creature's turn (or your own, alongside an action/Bonus Action). Only one Reaction can be taken per round, resetting at the start of your next turn. The Opportunity Attack is a Reaction available to all creatures.

    Ready [Action]

    Wait for a specific circumstance before acting. On your turn, choose the trigger and the response (an action or moving up to your Speed); when the trigger occurs, you may use your Reaction to respond or ignore it.

    Readying a spell (casting time of an action) expends its resources immediately but holds the effect, requiring Concentration until released or until the start of your next turn — if Concentration breaks, the spell fizzles with no effect.

    Resistance

    Damage of a type you have Resistance to is halved (round down), applied only once per instance of damage.

    Ritual

    A prepared spell with the Ritual tag can be cast as a Ritual, taking 10 minutes longer but expending no spell slot — meaning it can't be cast at a higher level this way.

    Round Down

    When dividing or multiplying, round down on a fraction — even exactly one-half — unless a rule specifically says to round up.

    Saving Throw

    Also called a save — an attempt to avoid or resist a threat, made only when a rule requires it (though you may choose to fail without rolling). If a target lacks the required ability score, it automatically fails.

    Search [Action]

    Make a Wisdom check to discern something not obvious.

    Shape-Shifting

    Effects like Wild Shape or Polymorph specify what happens to you. Ongoing effects (conditions, spells, curses) normally carry over between forms unless stated otherwise. You revert to your true form if you die.

    Short Rest

    A 1-hour period of downtime doing nothing more strenuous than reading, talking, eating, or standing watch. Requires at least 1 HP to start.

    Benefits: spend Hit Point Dice to regain HP (roll the die + Constitution modifier per die, minimum 1 HP each), and any features that recharge on a Short Rest do so.

    Interruptions: rolling Initiative, casting a non-cantrip spell, or taking damage — an interrupted Short Rest gives no benefits.

    Simultaneous Effects

    If multiple things happen at once on a turn, whoever's turn it is (player or DM) decides the order.

    Size

    Tiny, Small, Medium, Large, Huge, or Gargantuan. A creature's size determines the space it occupies in combat; an object's size affects its Hit Points.

    Skill

    A specialization tied to an ability check. Proficiency in a skill adds your Proficiency Bonus to associated checks.

    Speed

    The distance in feet a creature can move on its turn.

    Special Speeds. Burrow, Climb, Fly, and Swim Speed each have their own entry. With more than one, choose which to use as you move, switching mid-move by subtracting distance already moved from the new speed (if 0 or less, that speed can't be used this move).

    Changes to Speed. If Speed is increased/decreased, any special speed changes by the same amount for the same duration (e.g. Speed reduced to 0 also reduces Climb Speed to 0; Speed halved also halves Fly Speed).

    Spell

    A magical effect with the characteristics described under Spells in the rules.

    Spell Attack

    An attack roll made as part of a spell or other magical effect.

    Spellcasting Focus

    An object certain creatures can use in place of a spell's Material components, provided those materials aren't consumed and have no listed cost.

    Sphere [Area of Effect]

    Extends in straight lines from a point of origin outward in all directions, out to a specified radius. Unlike most shapes, the origin point IS included in the area.

    Stable

    A creature at 0 HP that isn't required to make Death Saving Throws.

    Stat Block

    Contains a monster's game statistics: Size, Creature Type, Alignment, AC/Initiative/HP, Speed, ability scores, skills, resistances/vulnerabilities, immunities, gear, senses, languages, CR (with XP and Proficiency Bonus), and its traits, actions, bonus actions, and reactions.

    Attack entries give whether it's melee or ranged, the attack bonus, reach/range, and effect on a hit. Save-based effects give the save type, DC, who must save, and the effects of failure/success. Damage is usually shown as both a static number and a die expression (e.g. "4 (1d4 + 2)") — the DM picks one, not both.

    Study [Action]

    Make an Intelligence check to recall an important piece of information from memory, a book, a clue, or another source.

    Suffocation [Hazard]

    A creature can hold its breath for 1 + Constitution modifier minutes (minimum 30 seconds). Once out of breath or choking, it gains 1 Exhaustion level at the end of each of its turns until it can breathe again, at which point all levels gained this way are removed.

    Surprise

    A creature caught unawares at the start of combat is surprised, giving it Disadvantage on its Initiative roll.

    Swimming

    Each foot of movement while swimming costs 1 extra foot (2 extra in Difficult Terrain) unless you have and use a Swim Speed. The DM may require a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check to move any distance in rough water.

    Swim Speed

    Lets you swim without the extra movement cost normally required.

    Target

    A creature or object targeted by an attack roll, forced to save by an effect, or selected to receive a spell's or other phenomenon's effects.

    Telepathy

    Lets a creature communicate mentally with another within range; the contacted creature needn't share a language but must understand at least one language or be telepathic itself.

    A telepath needn't see the target and can start/end contact freely (no action). Contact can't start, and is broken, if either party is Incapacitated, if the target leaves range, or if the telepath contacts someone else. A non-telepath can receive but not initiate telepathic messages, though it can reply once contact is established.

    Teleportation

    You disappear and reappear elsewhere instantly without moving through the intervening space; this doesn't use movement (unless stated otherwise) and never provokes Opportunity Attacks. All worn/carried equipment teleports with you; a creature you're merely touching doesn't, unless the effect says so. If the destination is occupied or blocked, you appear in the nearest unoccupied space of your choice instead.

    Temporary Hit Points

    Granted by certain effects; act as a buffer against losing real Hit Points.

    Tremorsense

    Pinpoints creatures/moving objects within range while both you and the target share contact with the same surface or liquid. Can't detect anything airborne, and doesn't count as sight.

    Truesight

    Within its range: see through normal and magical Darkness, see Invisible creatures/objects, see through visual illusions (auto-succeeding saves against them), see a transformed creature's or object's true form, and see into the Ethereal Plane.

    Unarmed Strike

    A punch, kick, headbutt, or similar blow — a melee attack against a target within 5 feet. Choose one effect:

    • Damage. Attack roll (Strength modifier + Proficiency Bonus); on a hit, 1 + Strength modifier Bludgeoning damage.
    • Grapple. Target makes a Strength or Dexterity save (its choice) vs. DC 8 + Strength modifier + Proficiency Bonus, or gains the Grappled condition. Only works on a target up to one size larger, with a free hand.
    • Shove. Same DC and save; on a failure you push the target 5 feet away or knock it Prone. Only works on a target up to one size larger.
    Unoccupied Space

    A space is unoccupied if no creature is in it and it isn't completely filled by objects.

    Utilize [Action]

    Used when interacting with an object requires an action on its own, rather than as part of another action (like drawing a sword as part of the Attack action).

    Vulnerability

    Damage of a type you're Vulnerable to is doubled, applied only once per instance of damage.

    Weapon

    An object in the Simple or Martial weapon category.

    Weapon Attack

    An attack roll made with a weapon.
